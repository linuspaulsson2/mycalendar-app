Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-05-24

Mycalendar ("the App") is a personal calendar app developed by Linus Paulsson. We respect your privacy. This policy explains what data the App handles.

Data we collect

We do not collect any personal data on our servers. All your information is stored either:

  • Locally on your device — app preferences, photos cached for display.
  • In your own iCloud account via Apple's iCloud and CloudKit. Only accessible by you and people you explicitly share with.

We have no servers, no analytics, and no third-party tracking.

Data the App accesses on your device

  • Calendar (EventKit) — to read and write events, birthdays, and activities you create. Required for core functionality.
  • Photo Library — only if you enable "Memories from last year". Used to display photos from the same month one year ago. Photos are never uploaded or shared outside your device.
  • Location — to fetch weather for your current location. Coordinates are sent to Apple WeatherKit and to Open-Meteo for forecast data. No location data is stored on our servers.
  • Notifications — for silent push notifications used to sync data between your own devices.

Data shared with third parties

  • Apple iCloud / CloudKit — used as the storage and sync layer. Apple's privacy policy applies.
  • Apple WeatherKit — for current weather. Apple's privacy policy applies.
  • Open-Meteo (open-meteo.com) — for weather forecast data. Coordinates are sent but not stored.

We do not sell, rent, or share your data with any other parties.

Family sharing

If you choose to share your data with a family member via the "Family" feature, the data is shared via Apple CloudKit's sharing infrastructure (CKShare). Only people you explicitly invite can see the shared data. You can revoke sharing at any time from within the App.

Children

The App is suitable for all ages and does not knowingly collect data from children. No accounts are created and no data is sent to our servers.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy occasionally. Material changes will be noted in the App's release notes.
